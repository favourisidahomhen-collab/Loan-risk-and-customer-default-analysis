## CREDITRUST LOAN PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The analysis evaluates the performance and risk profile of Creditrust’s loan portfolio using customer segmentation, credit quality, employment status, income level, loan purpose, and risk tier. The findings reveal a relatively high portfolio default rate of 46.47%, with significant exposure concentrated among high-risk borrowers, unemployed customers, and customers with poor credit quality.
The portfolio contains a total loan exposure of $218 million distributed across 150 customers, with high-risk exposure accounting for $17 million (7.93%) of the portfolio. The analysis further highlights that loan purpose, employment status, and credit score are major determinants of default behavior.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
* The loan portfolio totals $218 million, reflecting the institution’s overall lending exposure.
* High-risk loans account for $17 million (7.93%) of the portfolio, increasing the likelihood of financial losses.
* The portfolio has a high default rate of 46.47%, indicating poor repayment performance among borrowers.
* The institution serves 150 customers, meaning individual defaults can significantly affect portfolio stability.
* The average loan size is $1.45 million, suggesting high exposure concentration per borrower.
* Only 9.33% of customers are classified as high risk, yet they contribute heavily to portfolio defaults.
* A total of 70 customers have defaulted, highlighting substantial credit risk within the portfolio.
* 17.33% of borrowers had previous default history, which may point to gaps in credit assessment and underwriting processes.
* The average risk score of 30 indicates the portfolio falls within a medium-to-high credit risk category.

## Loan and Risk Distribution Analysis

### Employment Status and Risk Exposure
The analysis shows that unemployed customers exhibited the highest concentration of high-risk borrowers, while employed customers demonstrated the lowest risk exposure.
#### Insights
*	Employed customers recorded the lowest high-risk customer ratio at 6.6%, indicating stronger repayment reliability and financial stability. 
*	Self-employed customers showed moderate risk exposure with approximately $6M in high-risk loan disbursement. 
*	Unemployed customers had the highest proportion of high-risk borrowers at 11.3%, reflecting increased repayment uncertainty due to unstable income sources. 
*	Loan exposure among employed customers was substantially larger, suggesting the institution has greater confidence in salaried borrowers. 
#### Business Interpretation
Employment stability appears to strongly influence repayment performance. Customers with unstable or inconsistent income streams contribute more significantly to portfolio risk.

### Customer Income Distribution Analysis
* The customer base was primarily composed of medium- and high-income earners.
* Medium-income customers make up the largest share of the portfolio at 40.0%, indicating they represent the institution’s primary customer segment.
* High-income customers account for 39.3% of borrowers, showing a strong concentration of financially stable clients within the portfolio.
* Low-income customers represent 20.7% of total customers, forming the smallest customer segment in the portfolio.
* The customer base is relatively balanced between medium- and high-income groups, which may help diversify income-related credit risk.
* Shorter loan durations appear to place greater repayment pressure on borrowers, increasing the likelihood of default. Longer repayment periods may provide customers with better financial flexibility and improved repayment capability. The differences across income groups suggest that repayment capacity is closely linked to income stability and loan structure.
#### Insights
*	Medium-income earners represented the largest customer segment, indicating the institution’s primary target market. 
*	Low-income customers contributed disproportionately to high-risk classifications despite representing the smallest customer group. 
*	Approximately 57.1% of high-risk customers belonged to the low-income category. 
*	High-income earners displayed relatively lower risk concentration, suggesting stronger repayment capacity. 
Business Interpretation
* Lower-income customers are more financially vulnerable and more likely to experience repayment difficulties, increasing default exposure within the portfolio.

### Default Rate by Loan Purpose
* Car loans recorded the highest default rate at 52.3%, indicating this loan category carries the greatest repayment risk.
Business loans followed closely with a 50.0% default rate, suggesting significant financial challenges among business borrowers.
* Educational loans showed a 46.2% default rate, reflecting moderate-to-high repayment risk within this segment.
* Personal loans had the lowest default rate at 35.5%, making them the relatively safest loan category in the portfolio.
* Overall, default rates remain high across all loan purposes, highlighting broad credit risk concerns within the lending portfolio.
#### Insights
*	Car loans recorded the highest default rate, indicating potential weaknesses in borrower affordability assessment or asset financing policies. 
*	Business loans also showed elevated default levels, likely due to income volatility and business performance uncertainty. 
*	Personal loans exhibited the lowest default rate overall, despite some high-risk customers defaulting completely within this category. 
*	High-risk borrowers consistently demonstrated severe repayment issues across multiple loan categories. 
Segment-Specific Observations
*	All high-risk customers who obtained personal loans defaulted. 
*	High-risk employed customers defaulted entirely on car, educational, and personal loans. 
*	High-risk self-employed customers showed 100% default occurrence in personal loans. 
*	High-risk unemployed customers defaulted completely in car and business loans. 
#### Business Interpretation
Loan purpose significantly influences repayment behavior. Asset-financing and business-related loans appear more vulnerable to economic instability and borrower cash flow challenges.

### Default Rate by Credit Score
* Customers with Very Poor credit scores recorded the highest default rate at 65.9%, indicating severe repayment risk within this category.
* Borrowers with Poor and Good credit scores both showed a default rate of 41.7%, suggesting similar repayment challenges despite differences in credit quality.
* Customers classified as Fair credit risk had a 40.0% default rate, reflecting moderate repayment performance.
* Borrowers with Excellent credit scores recorded the lowest default rate at 30.4%, making them the most reliable customer segment.
* Overall, the analysis shows a strong relationship between credit score quality and loan repayment performance, with lower credit scores generally linked to higher default risk.
#### Insights
*	Customers with very poor credit scores demonstrated the highest default tendency, validating the effectiveness of credit score as a risk indicator. 
*	Default rates declined progressively as credit quality improved. 
*	Even customers with good and fair credit scores exhibited relatively high default rates, suggesting additional risk drivers beyond credit score alone. 
*	Customers with excellent credit scores still recorded a 30.4% default rate, which remains materially high for premium borrowers. 
#### Business Interpretation
* Credit scoring alone may not be sufficient for loan approval decisions. Other variables such as employment stability, prior default history, and income level should be integrated into risk assessment models.

### Default Rate by Risk Tier
* High-risk customers recorded the highest default rate at 71.4%, indicating a substantial likelihood of loan repayment failure within this segment.
* Medium-risk borrowers showed a 60.6% default rate, reflecting significant credit risk despite being classified below high risk.
* Low-risk customers had the lowest default rate at 26.2%, demonstrating comparatively stronger repayment behavior.
* The results highlight a clear relationship between risk tier classification and default performance, with default rates increasing as customer risk levels rise.
#### Insights
*	High-risk customers exhibited the highest default frequency, confirming the reliability of the institution’s risk classification framework. 
*	Medium-risk borrowers also contributed significantly to defaults, indicating a need for tighter monitoring. 
*	Low-risk customers maintained comparatively stronger repayment performance. 
*	Longer loan terms appeared to reduce default frequency among high-risk customers but increased default likelihood among medium-risk borrowers. 
#### Business Interpretation
The current risk-tier framework successfully differentiates borrower quality; however, medium-risk customers may require enhanced monitoring and stricter loan structuring policies.

## Key Insights
### Portfolio-Level Insights
*	The portfolio default rate of 46.47% is critically high and suggests substantial credit risk exposure. 
*	High-risk customers represent only 9.33% of borrowers but contribute disproportionately to portfolio defaults. 
*	The institution has concentrated exposure in high-value loans, increasing financial vulnerability to borrower default. 
Customer Risk Insights
*	Unemployed and low-income borrowers display the highest probability of default. 
*	Previous defaulters continue to pose elevated repayment risk. 
*	Credit score remains a strong predictor of repayment behavior, particularly among very poor-score customers. 
Loan Product Insights
*	Car and business loans are the most vulnerable loan categories. 
*	Personal loans show lower overall default rates but severe default concentration among high-risk borrowers. 
*	Loan purpose materially impacts repayment outcomes and should be incorporated more strongly into underwriting policies. 
Risk Management Insights
*	The current risk-tier classification effectively identifies high-risk borrowers. 
*	Medium-risk customers contribute heavily to defaults and may be underestimated within existing risk controls. 
*	The institution may benefit from stronger affordability checks and customer segmentation strategies. 
## Recommendations
### Strengthen Credit Underwriting Policies
*	Introduce stricter approval criteria for unemployed and low-income applicants. 
*	Increase minimum credit score thresholds for car and business loans. 
*	Implement enhanced affordability assessments before loan approval. 
### Improve Risk-Based Pricing
*	Apply higher interest rates or stricter collateral requirements to high-risk borrowers. 
*	Offer preferential terms to low-risk customers to encourage portfolio quality improvement. 
### Tighten Monitoring of Medium- and High-Risk Borrowers
*	Establish early warning systems for missed or delayed repayments. 
*	Conduct periodic portfolio reviews for medium-risk customers, who currently contribute heavily to defaults. 
### Reassess High-Default Loan Products
*	Review underwriting standards for car and business loans. 
*	Introduce tighter eligibility requirements or reduced exposure limits for high-risk applicants within these categories. 
### Reduce Exposure to Repeat Defaulters
*	Limit loan approvals for customers with previous default history unless supported by strong compensating factors such as stable employment or collateral. 
### Enhance Portfolio Diversification
*	Reduce concentration in high-risk and unemployed customer segments. 
*	Increase lending focus toward stable salaried and low-risk borrowers. 
### Implement Advanced Risk Modeling
*	Combine credit score, employment status, income level, loan purpose, and prior default history into a more robust predictive risk model. 
*	Introduce behavioral scoring techniques to improve default prediction accuracy. 

## Conclusion
The analysis reveals that Creditrust’s loan portfolio is currently exposed to elevated credit risk, driven primarily by high default rates among unemployed borrowers, low-income earners, high-risk customers, and customers with weak credit profiles. While the institution’s risk-tier framework effectively identifies higher-risk borrowers, the overall portfolio default rate remains significantly above acceptable lending benchmarks.
To improve portfolio quality and reduce financial exposure, the institution should strengthen underwriting standards, enhance borrower monitoring, reduce exposure to high-risk segments, and implement more advanced risk-based lending strategies.
